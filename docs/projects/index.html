Research Programs

Research projects and ongoing work

These programs reflect my long-term research agenda at the intersection of AI industrial economics , semiconductor strategy , and AI-enabled scientific discovery .
I aim to translate complex technological transitions into actionable frameworks for researchers, investors, and industry leaders.

1) The Physical AI Economics Project
Theme: From intangible AI to physical intelligence

Focus

A long-term research program examining how AI value is shifting from software-centric innovation toward physical infrastructure , advanced manufacturing , and energy-constrained intelligence systems .

Key Questions

  • What becomes “scarce” in the next decade: models, compute, or power?
  • Why does value increasingly accumulate in energy systems, fabs, and deployment capacity?
  • How will industrial constraints reshape AI valuation and competitive advantage?

Outputs (in progress)

  • Conceptual frameworks (e.g., The Physical AI Stack )
  • Essay series and research notes on hard-asset premium & industrial bottlenecks
  • Working paper drafts and visual knowledge maps
2) Semiconductor Capability Amplification Research
Theme: Capability–demand fit, bottlenecks, and strategic advantage

Focus

A research initiative exploring how equipment innovation , process capability , and supply chain architecture determine long-run advantage in advanced semiconductor ecosystems.

Key Questions

  • How do equipment-first dynamics shape technology trajectories?
  • Where are the real bottlenecks: lithography, packaging, power, yield learning?
  • Which capabilities amplify across product cycles and platform transitions?

Outputs (in progress)

  • Industry structure notes (foundry / equipment / OSAT / advanced packaging)
  • Framework diagrams for capability amplification and bottleneck migration
  • Comparative analysis for strategic positioning across value chains
3) AI-Driven Scientific Discovery Initiative
Theme: Mechanistic plausibility vs biological causation

Focus

An interdisciplinary program studying how AI transforms molecular discovery , drug development , and the industrialization of biology —while clarifying common evidence traps in AI-biotech narratives.

Key Questions

  • How should we separate plausibility (Level A) from causation (Level B)?
  • Where does AI genuinely de-risk discovery, and where does it inflate valuation?
  • What are practical evaluation frameworks for investors and R&D leaders?

Outputs (in progress)

  • Research notes on AlphaFold 3, molecular pipelines, and evidence hierarchies
  • Due-diligence frameworks for AI-enabled biotech investment claims
  • Case-based essays and visual models
4) Industrial Strategy & Decision Systems
Theme: Turning research into decision leverage (Applied Research)

Focus

Applied research on how organizations convert AI capability into measurable outcomes—through decision prioritization, governance, and operational deployment.

Key Questions

  • What does it truly mean to “know AI” inside an organization?
  • How do firms measure productivity gains without eroding trust?
  • What operating model turns AI tools into durable advantage?

Outputs (in progress)

  • Practical playbooks and diagnostic frameworks
  • Executive-level notes on AI adoption, governance, and confidentiality
  • Cross-industry synthesis for leadership decision-making

Collaboration

  • Research partnerships (working papers, conceptual frameworks, datasets)
  • Industry strategy discussions (AI infrastructure, semiconductors, industrial AI)
  • Academic exchange (EDBA/PhD-level research development and publication)
